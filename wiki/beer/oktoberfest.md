## Summary

Oktoberfest beer — traditionally the **Märzen** style — is an amber, malt-forward bottom-fermented lager originally brewed in March (März) in Bavaria, cold-conditioned through summer, and consumed at autumn harvest festivals. It is defined by [[vienna-malt]] and [[munich-malt]] in the grain bill, producing a rich amber colour (7–15 EBC), moderate bitterness, and a smooth, bready malt character.

## Explanation

### Historical Context

The name "Märzen" comes from German for March — the month when the beer was traditionally brewed:
- Before modern refrigeration, summer was too warm for safe lager fermentation
- March was the last month cold enough for bottom fermentation
- Large batches were brewed and stored in cold cellars through summer
- Consumed at harvest festivals in autumn — most famously, Munich's Oktoberfest

With refrigeration, Märzen can now be brewed year-round, but the seasonal tradition persists.

### Characteristics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Colour | 7–15 EBC (amber to copper) |
| ABV | ~5.0–6.0% |
| Bitterness | 18–25 IBU (moderate, balanced) |
| Appearance | Clear, amber, dense white head |
| Flavour | Rich malt, honey, bread crust, clean finish |
| Body | Medium to full |

### Grain Bill

The style is defined by amber base malts:
- **Vienna Malt** (30–50%): Light honey, biscuit, amber colour
- **Munich Malt** (30–60%): Rich malt, honey, deeper amber
- Small additions of Pilsner malt for lightening or Caramel 50 for depth
- No dark malts — the colour comes entirely from kilning intensity of base malts

See [[marzen]] for grain bill examples.

### Modern vs Traditional Oktoberfest

Two distinct styles are marketed as "Oktoberfest" today:

| Version | EBC | ABV | Character |
|---------|-----|-----|-----------|
| **Märzen** (traditional) | 7–14 | 5.0–6.0% | Amber, malt-forward, rich |
| **Oktoberfest Lager** (modern) | 3–5 | 5.0–5.8% | Pale golden, lighter, more hop presence |

The modern pale "Oktoberfest" was introduced by major Munich breweries in the 1950s–1970s as consumer preference shifted toward lighter beers. Traditional Märzen-style Oktoberfest is now sold as "Wies'n" (meadow beer) in some Munich festivals.

### Viking Recipe Context

The Viking Malt handbook includes a **Märzen/Vienna Lager** recipe using:
- Viking Ale Malt + Viking Munich Malt
- Saaz hops (noble hop variety)
- Lager yeast (Saflager W-34/70 equivalent)
- Target: ~12°P OG, ~2.5°P FG → ~4.8% ABV
