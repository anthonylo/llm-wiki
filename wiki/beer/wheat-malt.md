## Summary

Wheat Malt is malted wheat used as a partial grain bill substitute for barley malt. It contributes head foam stability, haze, and a soft, slightly tart, bready flavour. It is the defining ingredient of German Hefeweizen, Belgian witbier, and American wheat beers, and increasingly appears in New England IPA (NEIPA) for haze and body.

## Explanation

### Characteristics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Colour | 3–5 EBC |
| Extract yield | ~82–84% dry basis (higher than barley — more starch, less husk) |
| Diastatic power | 150–250 WK |
| Protein | 12–14% (higher than barley) |
| Husk | Minimal — causes lautering issues at high proportions |
| Flavour | Soft, slightly tart, bready, fresh |

### How Wheat Malt Differs from Barley Malt

| Feature | Barley Malt | Wheat Malt |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Husk | Thick, aids lautering | Thin/absent — increases lautering difficulty |
| Protein | ~10–11% | ~12–14% — more haze-forming proteins |
| Beta-glucan | Lower | Higher — contributes to body |
| Flavour | Malty, biscuity | Bready, slightly sour |
| Extract yield | ~79–81% | ~82–84% (huskless = more starch) |

High wheat grists (>60%) require rice hulls added to the lauter tun to prevent stuck sparges.

### Why Use Wheat Malt?

1. **Head foam stability**: Wheat proteins (especially LTP1) create exceptionally stable, dense foam
2. **Haze**: Wheat proteins + polyphenols produce permanent biological haze (desired in Hefeweizen, NEIPA)
3. **Body and mouthfeel**: Beta-glucans and proteins add fullness
4. **Flavour**: Soft, slightly tart wheaty character distinct from barley

### Viking Wheat Malt Products

- **Viking Wheat Malt** — standard malted wheat, pale, used as ~40–70% of grist in wheat beer styles
- **Viking Red Active Malt** — speciality active malt (partially diastatic) used in low-carb and active beer recipes; distinct from standard wheat malt

### Typical Use by Style

| Beer Style | Wheat % | Role |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Hefeweizen | 50–70% | Defines style; haze + foam |
| Belgian Witbier | 30–50% | + unmalted wheat; haze, body |
| American Wheat | 30–50% | Clean version; less yeast character |
| NEIPA | 10–25% | Haze and protein body |
| Berliner Weisse | 50–70% | Light sour wheat beer |
