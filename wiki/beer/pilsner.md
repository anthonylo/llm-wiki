## Summary

Pilsner is a pale lager beer that originated in Bohemia, in the city of Pilsen. Known as the 'mother' of all pale lagers, pilsner was the first golden, clear lager beer and is renowned for its fine hop aroma, strong bitterness, and smooth malty taste due to Pilsen's soft brewing water.

## Explanation

### Background

Pilsner originated in Bohemia, specifically in the city of Pilsen, and was the first golden, clear lager beer. It is known for its soft brewing water, which contributes significantly to its smooth taste. Over half of the beer drunk in Germany is pilsner, making it one of the most popular beer styles worldwide.

### Characteristics

Pilsner was originally famous for its fine hop aroma and strong bitterness. Its golden color and moderate alcohol content, combined with its slightly lower final attenuation, give it a distinctive smooth malty taste. Modern pilsners have expanded in range, with lighter, less-hopped versions now considered ordinary lagers.

### Typical Analysis

- **Original gravity**: 11-12 °Plato
- **Alcohol content**: 4.5-5.2% volume
- **Color**: 6-12 °EBC
- **Bitterness**: 25-40 BU
