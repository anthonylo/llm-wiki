## Summary

Pale Ale Malt is a base malt kilned slightly higher than Pilsner malt (90–100°C vs 80°C), producing a creamier, biscuity flavour profile. It is the traditional backbone of British ales, IPAs, and American Pale Ales, providing more malt character than Pilsner malt while retaining full diastatic power.

## Explanation

### Characteristics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Colour | 4–7 EBC |
| Extract yield | ~79–81% dry basis |
| Diastatic power | 240–320 WK |
| Protein | 10–11% |
| Flavour | Biscuity, slightly nutty, fresh bread |

### Pale Ale Malt vs Pilsner Malt

| Feature | Pilsner Malt | Pale Ale Malt |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Kilning temp | ~80°C | ~90–100°C |
| EBC colour | 2–4 | 4–7 |
| Flavour | Neutral, clean | Biscuity, fuller |
| Primary use | Lager, Pilsner | Ale, IPA, Bitter |
| Maillard character | Minimal | Light-moderate |

### Beer Styles Using Pale Ale Malt

- **English Bitter / Pale Ale**: 90–100% Pale Ale Malt base
- **American Pale Ale (APA)**: Pale Ale Malt + hop-forward additions
- **India Pale Ale (IPA)**: Pale Ale Malt base to handle intense dry hopping
- **Session ales**: Pale Ale Malt at lower gravity for easy-drinking

### Viking Product

**Viking Golden Ale Malt** — Viking's pale ale–style base malt, ~5–7 EBC, used in Viking APA, Viking Beer Lager, and several hop-forward recipes.
