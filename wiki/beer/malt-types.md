## Summary

Malts are the primary colour, flavour, and fermentable sugar source in beer. They range from pale base malts (providing most of the fermentable extract and all the enzymes) to deeply roasted malts (contributing colour and roasted flavours but no fermentable material). Selecting the right combination of malts is the brewer's primary creative tool.

## Explanation

### Malt Categories

#### 1. Base Malts (Pale Brewing Malts)

The foundation of any beer recipe. Base malts:
- Provide the bulk of fermentable extract (80–82% extract yield)
- Contain sufficient diastatic power (enzymes) to convert their own starch and adjunct starches
- Kilned at relatively low temperatures to preserve enzyme activity
- Form 60–100% of the malt bill

| Malt | Colour (EBC) | Character | Common Use |
|------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **Pilsner Malt** | 3.3–4.3 | Very pale, neutral, light malt | Pilsners, lagers, all styles |
| **Pale Ale Malt** | 4–7 | Slightly more malt character than Pilsner | Ales, IPAs, APAs |
| **Vienna Malt** | 7–10 | Light amber, honey/biscuit notes | Vienna lager, Märzen, amber ales |
| **Munich Light** | ~16 | Rich malt aroma, amber | Märzen, Bock, dark ales |
| **Wheat Malt** | 3.5–6.5 | Bready, slightly acidic, no husk | Wheat beers (min. 50%); foam enhancement (2–5%) |

#### 2. Caramel Malts

Produced from green malt by stewing then roasting (see [[malt-production]]). The endosperm is caramelised — converting starch to unfermentable caramel-like compounds. Key properties:
- **No diastatic power** — cannot convert starch; must be used with base malts
- Sweet, toffee, caramel, raisin, plum flavours depending on colour
- Add body, colour, and head retention
- Typically 5–20% of malt bill

| Malt | Colour (EBC) | Character |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Caramel Malt Light (~10) | 8–12 | Honey, light caramel |
| Caramel Malt 50 | 40–60 | Toffee, caramel, amber colour |
| Caramel Malt Dark (~200) | 150–250 | Dark fruit, raisin, plum |
| CaraBody Malt | medium | Body enhancement, head retention |
| Dextrin Malt | very pale | Non-fermentable dextrins; body and mouthfeel |

#### 3. Dark Brewing Malts

Extended kilning of well-modified base malt. Higher colour and flavour intensity, lower enzyme activity:

| Malt | Colour (EBC) | Character |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Munich Dark | 20–30 | Rich malt, bread crust |
| Brown Malt | 50–80 | Biscuit, nutty, brown bread |
| Amber Malt | 30–70 | Dry biscuit, bread |

#### 4. Roasted Malts and Products

Very high temperatures (>200°C) in a roasting drum. No enzyme activity. Used in small quantities (1–10%) for colour and flavour only:

| Product | Raw Material | Colour (EBC) | Character |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Roasted Barley** | Unmalted barley | 900–1400 | Dry coffee, bitter, astringent |
| **Black Malt** | Pale malt | 1100–1400 | Coffee, chocolate, harsh if over-used |
| **Chocolate Malt** | Pale malt | 800–900 | Chocolate, coffee, smooth |
| **Carafa Special** | Dehusked barley | 900–1300 | Coffee/chocolate without harshness (no husk astringency) |

#### 5. Specialty Malts

Viking Malt's portfolio includes specialty malts with unique character:
- **Viking Red Active Malt**: imparts red/amber hue; used in red ales, amber lagers
- **Viking Golden Ale Malt**: smooth, golden character
- **Viking Cookie Malt**: biscuit, cookie-like flavour
- **Viking Oat Malt**: adds silky mouthfeel; popular in NEIPA and stouts
- **Viking Pilsner Zero Malt**: very light colour (for low-colour precision)
- **Viking Smoked Malt**: peat or wood-smoked base malt

### Matching Malt to Beer Style

| Beer Style | Primary Malt | Supporting Malts |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Pilsner | Pilsner Malt | ± Dextrin (body) |
| American IPA | Pale Ale Malt | ± Dextrin, small Caramel |
| Vienna Lager | Vienna Malt | Munich Light |
| Märzen | Munich Light | Vienna |
| Stout | Pilsner or Pale Ale | Roasted Barley, Chocolate, Caramel |
| Porter | Pale Ale | Chocolate, Caramel, Crystal |
| Hefeweizen | Wheat Malt (50%+) | Pilsner Malt |
| NEIPA | Pale Ale / Pilsner | Oat Malt, Dextrin |
| Baltic Porter | Multiple dark malts | Caramel, Chocolate, Munich |

### Malt Flavour Wheel Concept

The handbook presents flavour profiles evaluated organoleptically:
- **Roasty**: coffee, dark chocolate → high-temp roasted malts
- **Caramel/Sweet**: toffee, honey, raisin → caramel malts
- **Malty/Bready**: fresh bread, biscuit → Munich, Vienna malts
- **Neutral**: light malt, cereal → Pilsner, pale malts
- **Astringent/Bitter**: husk tannins → can come from over-roasted or poorly-processed malts

Flavour intensity increases non-linearly with roasting temperature. Even small percentages of dark malts dominate beer colour and flavour.

## Contradictions

> **Malt bill flexibility**: The handbook presents malt recommendations per style as guidelines, not rigid rules. Commercial pilsners often add non-malt adjuncts (corn, rice) for cost and lightness — a practice the handbook doesn't emphasise but is common globally. The Viking Malt handbook focuses on all-malt brewing.
