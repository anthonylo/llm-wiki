## Summary

Munich Malt is a kilned base malt characterised by deep amber colour (14–20 EBC) and a rich, honey-malt flavour. It sits between Vienna malt and caramel malts on the colour/flavour spectrum, and defines the malt-forward character of Märzen, Bock, and Dunkel (dark lager) beer styles.

## Explanation

### Characteristics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Colour | 14–20 EBC |
| Extract yield | ~77–79% dry basis |
| Diastatic power | 100–200 WK (lower than pale malt — still enzymatically active) |
| Protein | 11–12% |
| Flavour | Honey, malt, bread crust, light amber sweetness |

Munich malt is still a **base malt** — it retains enough diastatic power to self-convert, unlike specialty malts. At higher proportions (>50% of grist), it can self-mash without additional base malt. Below 100 WK total mash diastatic power, conversion is incomplete.

### Flavour Chemistry

Munich malt is kilned at 100–110°C (vs 80°C for Pilsner malt), which drives more intense Maillard reaction products. The result:
- **Melanoidins**: brown colour compounds contributing amber hue
- **Honey, toffee, fresh bread**: hallmark flavour markers
- **More body**: melanoidins contribute mouthfeel and head stability

### Typical Use in Recipes

| Beer Style | Munich Malt % | Role |
|-----------|--------------|------|
| Märzen (Oktoberfest) | 50–70% | Defines malt character |
| Bock | 60–80% | Rich malt base |
| Dunkel (Dark Lager) | 40–60% | Amber colour and body |
| English Brown Ale | 20–30% | Malt complexity |
| Irish Red Ale | 15–25% | Amber colour, malt sweetness |

### Munich Malt vs Vienna Malt

| Feature | Vienna Malt | Munich Malt |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Colour | 7–10 EBC | 14–20 EBC |
| Kilning temp | ~95°C | ~105°C |
| Flavour intensity | Lighter honey, biscuit | Richer honey, more malt |
| Self-mashing | Yes | Yes (margins tighter) |

### Viking Product

**Viking Munich Malt** — Viking's standard Munich malt at ~14–18 EBC. Used in Viking Dark Red Lager, grain bills for Märzen-style beers, and mixed specialty bills.
