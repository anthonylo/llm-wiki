## Summary

Malt extracts are concentrated liquid or dry products produced from pre-mashed wort. They allow brewers to skip the mashing step entirely, dissolving directly into hot water to produce fermentable wort. Viking Malt's sister company Senson produces a range of malt extracts (branded Maltax) from very pale to dark, for brewing, baking, and beverage applications.

## Explanation

### What Are Malt Extracts?

Malt extracts are produced by:
1. Mashing malt conventionally (converting starch to sugars)
2. Lautering and collecting the wort
3. Concentrating by vacuum evaporation → **liquid malt extract (LME)** (~20% moisture)
4. Optionally spray-drying → **dry malt extract (DME)** (<5% moisture)

The result: a shelf-stable, standardised product with known fermentable content, colour, and flavour.

### Types of Malt Extract (Maltax Range)

| Product | Colour | Description | Applications |
|---------|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Maltax 10** (Light) | Light brown | Pleasant malty, sweet flavour; good source of fermentable sugars | Brewing, baking, beverages |
| **Maltax 800 Gold** (Golden) | Reddish-brown | Deep reddish-golden colour; caramel, malty notes | Amber beers, coloured breads, crust |
| **Maltax 200 Clear** (Dark Red) | Deep reddish | Complex dark malt flavour | Dark lagers, porter-style beverages |
| **Finlandia Russian Kvas** | Dark | Rye-based extract; kvas character | Porter-style, specialty brewing |

**Non-diastatic**: Most malt extracts have no residual enzyme activity — they are fully converted. Cannot be used to mash additional grain.

**Diastatic extracts**: Some Maltax products retain enzyme activity for use as partial mash supplements.

### Extract Brewing Recipes (from Viking Handbook)

**Dark Red Lager** (full extract recipe):
- Grist: 9% Maltax 10 + 1% granulated sugar + 1% Maltax 800 Gold + 89% water
- Boil 60 min, lactic acid for pH 5.0
- Hops: Saaz (bitter + aroma)
- Result: ABV 4.7%, Colour 53 EBC, Bitterness 26 BU

**Porter** (extract recipe):
- Grist: 13% Maltax 10 + 4% Finlandia Russian Kvas + 2% Maltax 200 Clear + 1.5% granulated sugar + 79.5% water
- Result: ABV 6.1%, Colour 19 EBC, Bitterness 34 BU, "plummy, winy, chocolate, sherry-like"

**Lager Gluten Free** (extract, gluten-free):
- 10% Maltax 10 + 2% granulated sugar + 88% water
- No malt = no gluten → gluten-free beer
- Result: ABV 5.1%, Colour 12 EBC, Bitterness 22 BU

**Alcohol Free Lager** (extract):
- 5% Maltax 10 + 95% water
- Very low OG → very low ABV (0.03 vol%)
- Result: ABV 0.03%, Colour 8 EBC, Bitterness 21 BU

### Advantages of Extract Brewing

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| No mash equipment | Skip lauter tun, mash paddle, temperature control |
| Consistency | Standardised extract → repeatable results |
| Speed | Brew day significantly shorter |
| Gluten-free possible | Select gluten-free base materials |
| Small-scale friendly | Suitable for homebrew, nano-brewing, R&D |
| Shelf-stable | Can store extracts for months/years |

### Limitations vs All-Grain Brewing

| Limitation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Less control | Fixed fermentability and character from extract supplier |
| Colour precision | Harder to hit exact target EBC |
| Freshness | Extract oxidises over time; affects flavour |
| Cost | More expensive per kg than raw malt at scale |
| Caramelisation risk | Concentrated extract scorches easily when added to hot water |

### Senson's Role

Senson is Viking Malt's sister company dedicated to extract production. The Maltax range is marketed as "natural food ingredients" — suitable for brewing, baking, and confectionery. The Viking Malt Handbook presents extract brewing as a complementary offering to traditional all-grain brewing, particularly useful for specialty and experimental beers.

### Maltax Sour Extract

A specialty product: **Maltax Sour extract** provides instant sourness without traditional souring bacteria (lactobacillus, pediococcus). Used in the Viking Bubble Sour recipe to achieve lactic sourness quickly and conveniently — circumventing the traditional 24–72 hour souring process.

## Contradictions

> **Extract vs all-grain taste**: The craft brewing community often claims all-grain beer is superior in flavour to extract-brewed equivalents. The Viking handbook presents extract brewing neutrally as a valid production method. For professional and homebrewing at small scale, the difference may be negligible with fresh, high-quality extracts; at scale or with aged extracts, differences are more pronounced.
