## Summary

Hefeweizen (German: "yeast wheat") is a top-fermented German wheat beer characterised by a hazy appearance, dense white foam, and a distinctive aroma profile of banana (isoamyl acetate) and clove (4-vinyl guaiacol) — produced by a specific POF+ yeast strain. It uses 50–70% [[wheat-malt]] in the grist and is served unfiltered with suspended yeast.

## Explanation

### Defining Characteristics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Colour | 4–10 EBC (pale to light amber) |
| ABV | ~4.5–5.5% |
| Bitterness | Low (10–15 IBU) — not hop-forward |
| Haze | Permanent biological haze (yeast + wheat proteins) |
| Carbonation | High |
| Flavour | Banana, clove, light bread, citrus |

### The Yeast Is Everything

Hefeweizen character is defined primarily by the yeast, not the malt or hops:

- **Strain**: POF+ ale yeast (*Saccharomyces cerevisiae* with phenolic off-flavour gene)
- **4-Vinyl guaiacol (4VG)**: clove character — the POF+ gene converts ferulic acid to 4VG
- **Isoamyl acetate**: banana character — ester produced at warm fermentation
- **Ferulic acid rest**: Mashing at ~43–45°C for 15–20 min releases ferulic acid from wheat, maximising 4VG clove character

Fermentation temperature controls the **banana/clove balance**:
- Warmer fermentation (~22°C+): More banana (isoamyl acetate dominant)
- Cooler fermentation (~18–20°C): More clove (4VG dominant)

### Grain Bill

Typical Hefeweizen grain bill:
- 50–70% [[wheat-malt]] — haze, foam, soft flavour
- 30–50% Pilsner or Pale Ale malt — enzymatic backbone (wheat malt has lower diastatic power at high %)
- Optional: small pale caramel addition for slight colour

**Lautering note**: High wheat content (>50%) = minimal husk → stuck sparges. Rice hulls added to improve lauterability.

### Hefeweizen vs Related Styles

| Style | Key Difference |
|-------|---------------|
| **Hefeweizen** | Unfiltered, POF+ yeast, banana/clove |
| **Kristallweizen** | Filtered clear version — no haze |
| **Dunkelweizen** | Dark wheat beer — Munich/caramel malts add amber colour and malt character |
| **Weizenbock** | Strong wheat beer (7–9% ABV) |
| **Belgian Witbier** | Spiced (coriander, orange peel), different yeast; less banana/clove |
| **American Wheat** | Clean neutral fermentation, no POF+ phenolics |

### Viking Recipe Notes

The Viking Malt handbook references a **Viking Orange Wheat Ale** using:
- Wheat malt
- Safale US-05 (clean, non-POF+ yeast) → American Wheat character, not traditional Hefeweizen phenolics
- Orange flavour addition

Traditional Hefeweizen is produced with dedicated POF+ strains (e.g., Wyeast 3068, White Labs WLP300, Lallemand Munich Classic).
